### Practical Advice

- **Suica/Pasmo**: Top up ¥3,000–¥5,000 for the day; covers train, subway, and even convenience‑store purchases.
- **Language**: Most signage in Shinjuku Gyoen and Shibuya is bilingual; staff at major stations usually speak basic English.
- **Weather**: Carry a compact umbrella or rain jacket; Shinjuku Gyoen’s ponds can be slippery when wet.
- **Crowds**: If you prefer a quieter garden experience, aim to arrive right at opening (9:00 am) and leave before the lunchtime rush; the afternoon is still pleasant but busier.
- **Accessibility**: Shinjuku Gyoen has paved paths and wheelchair‑rental services (¥1,000/day). Shibuya Crossing and Hachiko Statue are fully accessible, though the scramble can be challenging during peak flow—consider using the nearby **Shibuya Station** elevators to cross via the underground passage if needed.
